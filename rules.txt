Rule book
BGMI
This is a squad-based match (Solo participation
not allowed).
Each team must have 4 players only.
All matches will be played in Custom Room.
Emulators 
are 
strictly 
not 
allowed 
(Mobile
devices only).
Use of hacks, mods, or cheats will result in
immediate disqualification.
Match details (Room ID & Password) will be
shared before the match.
Late entry will not be allowed once the match
starts.
Team 
names 
and 
in-game 
names 
must 
be
appropriate.
Final decision of the organizers will be binding.

Rule book
PPT PRESENTATION 
Participation can be individual or in a team (max
3 members).
Presentation time: 5–7 minutes + 2 minutes Q&A.
PPT 
should 
contain 
minimum 
8 
slides 
and
maximum 12 slides.
Problem Statement will be given.
Participants must bring their PPT in pen drive or
email backup.
Use 
of 
plagiarized 
content 
will 
lead 
to
disqualification.
Evaluation will be based on:
            Content quality
            Presentation skills
            Slide design
            Time management
Decision of judges will be final.

Rule book
AI PROMPT
This is an individual event.
Participants 
will 
be 
given 
a 
task/problem
statement on the spot.
Only AI tools allowed by the organizers can be
used.
Time limit: 15–20 minutes.
Prompts must be original and created during the
event.
Copying prompts from others is not allowed.
Judging criteria:
          Prompt creativity
          Accuracy of output
          Efficiency of prompt
Organizers’ decision will be final.

Rule book
TECHNICAL QUIZ 
Participation can be individual or team of 2
members.
Quiz consists of multiple rounds.
Questions will be based on:
           Core engineering subjects
           Basic programming
           General technical knowledge
Use of mobile phones or smart devices is strictly
prohibited.
Each question must be answered within the given
time limit.
In case of a tie, tie-breaker questions will be
conducted.
The quiz master’s decision will be final.

Rule book
CIRCUIT DEBUGGING 
Participation can be individual or team of 2
members.
Participants will be given a faulty circuit or
circuit diagram.
Time limit: 20–30 minutes.
Use of only provided components and tools is
allowed.
No external help or internet access is permitted.
Marks will be awarded based on:
          Correct identification of fault
          Proper circuit connection
          Time taken to debug
Damaging components intentionally will lead to
disqualification.
Judges’ decision will be final.

Rule book
TRIVIA
The event will be conducted in online mode.
Questions will be displayed by screen sharing on
the whiteboard.
Participants 
must 
carefully 
read/watch 
and
submit their answers within the given time.
Participants must maintain discipline and avoid
any unfair means.
Use of Google, AI tools, or external help is strictly
prohibited.
The decision of the judges/organizing committee
will be final and binding.
Each round will have a specific time limit.
Points will be awarded for correct answers only.
In case of a tie, a tie-breaker question will be
asked.
